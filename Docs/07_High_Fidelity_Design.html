← New question Math "Why does increasing a in y = ax² make the parabola narrower?"
Stretch factor — a 2.0
Vertical shift — c 0.0

A larger value of a stretches the curve vertically, making it look narrower.

Multiplying x² by a larger a pulls every point further from the x-axis for the same horizontal step. The graph rises more steeply — visually narrower — but it's still a parabola. Drag the slider left to see it widen, right to tighten.
